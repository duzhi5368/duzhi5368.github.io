How to accomplish our indie game: NOT motivation but discipline.

How to accomplish our indie game: NOT motivation but discipline.

Hey, guys, welcome. This is Frankie, I am practicing writing diary in English. Today I’m gonna to talk about how to accomplish a indie game consistently. As you know, I became a solo indie game developer last for one and a half year, I always think about how to stay motivated and I searched a lot on YouTube even on itch. Finally, I found a very unique take on this, and I really think that this is going to help other indie game developer. But this is not a “how to stay motivated” article, I want to prove you why motivation is not what we need to finish our game, what we actually need is probably not what we think.

Okay, so if you’ve been visited my Wechat Moments or QQZone before, then you’ve heard me say again and again, “Indie video game development is an ART.”, I feel very strongly about this, and as in any other art form, you’re going to hear a lot of talk about motivation how to stay motivated, how to get those creative juices flowing, how to overcome a writer’s block, I Googled it apparently that’s a thing. But this becomes a very big problem when we’re tring to turn our art into a viable business, we’re not just making games for the fun and for the joy of it, we ARE doing it for those things, but we’re also doing it in hopes of making some money. This is not just an art, it’s also a business. And if I wanna the freedom to be able to create games for the rest of my life, then I’am going to have to bring in enough money to be able to sustain that, and when I’m relying on something as fickle as motivation things can get very stressful and this is because motivation is not completely within my control. Yeah, there are definitely things that I can do to help me to stay motivated, but despite that, there are going to be days where I know I should be working on my game, and I just don’t feel like it. And honestly it feels like the worst possible thing that I could do, and I’d rather do anything else there doesn’t even have to be a trigger or an event that makes me feel that way. I can just wake up and feel unmotivated that day. I am a human being, we are human beings, our lives, our moods, our energy levels, our motivation to pursue our goals, it all changes day to day. And this is normal. And I gonna tell you it yould be so easy to come in at this point and start talking to you about discipline. I’ve thinked about that before and I DO need discipline to be successful in this industry. But when we think about the word “discipline”, what does that mean to you? To me it means powering through and doing the things I don’t feel like doing, or just plain not doing the things that actually really want to do but aren’t necessarily for me. At least that is what discipline used to mean to me.

If you are also a smoker who wants to quit really badly, you will know we’ve tried and tried and we’ve just can’t seem to do it. Especially if you are a non-smoker, it’s really easy to sit there and say, “Why don’t you just not pick up a cigarette? If you don’t want to pick up a cigarette and smoke it then don’t pick up a cigarette and smoke it.” This ovbiously is not the full picture though bacuase that smoker like me had tried and failed to quit multiple times now. So you’re probably want to know what does this have to do with motivation and game development? We’re getting there, the reality is that discipline in the context of if you want to do something then just do it. That’s going to make you feel like crap and it doesn;t just work like that. This is because of neural pathways in you brain, whenever you take an action, you are either creating a new neural pathway. If it’s a new activity that you’ve never done, or you’re following a pre-existing neural pathway, and when you do something enough times (like driving to work) that neural pathway becomes so entranched in your brain, that you no longer need to consciously think about it in order to do it. You can drive to work and not remember the drive afterwards, that’s because you’ve done it so many times now, that your subconscious mind is able to handle that task freeing up conscious mind to think about more important things. Now, back to our smoker, let’s say whenever they take a break at work, they have a smoke and on the drive home and after they finish dinner and the next morning after they wake up and make coffer, there are triggers or events sprinkled throughout that smoker’s day, where there are deep neural pathways which makes them desperately want to smoke. You have a easy time not smoking when you are a non-smoker, but the smoker has triggers throughout their day. In fact, their brain are getting flooded with dopamine, the feel-good hormone before they have a smoke. Which means their brains are literally anticipating the act of smoking before they’ve even done it. The brain rewarding them just for the thought of wanting one, so what does this mean? It means that simply buckling down and being disciplined. I.E, just don’t have a smoke, it’s very unlikely to work. They have triggers all over their nomal daily life, that is going to make them desperatelt want to smoke. This doesn’t mean they can’t quit cold turkey, it just makes it very very unlikely. They might have a lot of motivation to quit, maybe they had a health scare, or maybe they’ve been told that they’re going to die if they can’t find a way to quit. And yet some people can’t quit, motivation is not the problem now, this is an extreme example and it’s kind of enchanced because smoking involves addictive substances. But, when we look at things from that angle, it’s really easy to see that the motivation is not the answer. They don’t want to die they don’t want to get sick, they’ve told you they desperately want to quit, they have plenty of motivation to quit, so why can’t they, why do they have so much trouble? Well, a big part of the reason is because of their habits, and because of the neural pathways that their brain wants them to follow. Our brain is a highly evolobed tool that is always looking for ways to automate certain tasks, and when we’ve done something enought times as I said, it becomes a habit. Which means our subconscious mind is handling us doing that task more than our conscious mind is. This is really really hard to fight against, so motivation is not what’s needed here, what’s needed are good habits. Good habits that are going to help us in accomplishing our goal of finishing our game. What if we were able to make a habit of working on our game every single weekday an hour before work, we don’t need to fight it, we don’t even really think about it. We just wake up make a coffee and go work on our game for an hour. So maybe we’ve been asking ourselves the wrong quetions, maybe it’s not how do I stay motivated to finish my game, maybe it’s how can I establish good game development habits.

When we step back and relize starting a new projet is always fun, there’s nothing but opportunity things not gotten out of control or bloated or messy yet. So we enjoy starting new projects then it’s easy to see why it’s so difficult to push through and keep working on the game we actually want to create. Creating new mechanic after new mechanic is way more fun than having to do things like optimization and other tasks that we would need to worry about for a commercial game. Finding motivation for new projects is easy, so if we’ve been struggling to work on our game consistently, it’s not because we’re lazy and it’s not because we need to find a way to motivate ourselves to keep working on it. It’s just likely that we don’t have any habits in place to help us to continue working on our game, even when we don’t want to. What I’ve come to learn slowly but surely over the years is that, a disciplined person is not this superhuman person with like massive willpower that just powers through and does the things that need to get done, and can just resist all temptations and distractions. A disciplined person is someone who has taken a little bit of time and done a little bit of work to optimize their environment for their day or their life, in order to achieve the things that they want to achieve, they optimize things so that making good choices is easy to do. For example, if you want to eat less junk food and you want to eat more healthy, hide the jar of candies that you keep on your kitchen island and replace it with a bowl of fruit instead. If you want to watch less TV in bed, then keep your remote in the living room instead of on your nightstand. If we want to work on our game every single day, what can we do to make that easy for ourselves? what can we do to reward ourselves for doing the work? how can we make it easy and how can we make it fun？The huge difference between this and motivation is that optimizing our environment is completely within our control, our motivation level isn’t.

Sometimes we’re just not going to want to work on our game, that’s okay. But if we have a goal of working on our game every single dat, what can we do to make it easy? Maybe we have a little candy jar on our desk and if we sit down to work on our game just for five minutes a day, the we allow ourselves to take a candy or two out of the jar, and no other time ever. And if we’ve been on our computer for five minutes doing some work,it’s very likely that we’re going to want to continue working longer than that, but make that five minute each day a habit and then reward ourselves for it. That might sound a little bit stupid, but if we do that, hat are we doing? We are training our brain to expact a reward for doing the thing that we don’t feel like doing. If we do that consistently, our brain is going to start to expect it, and now we’ve got a neural pathway set up that “associates working with candy” which might just make it a difficult for us in the long run. This is obviously just one example, there are so many things that we can do. If our goal is work on our indie game every single day, then our goal should be to establish a habit of working on our game every single day, not to find the motivation which will allow us to work on our game every single day. This can extend into all sorts of tiny little habits that will make us more efficient at what we do.

Those neural pathways we’ve been talking about they have certain triggers and our environment is a part of those triggers, so if we have an office that we only do work. In our mind is going to start associating that room with getting work done. If we’re working in bed or in the kitchen, we’re gonna have all sorts of other triggers firing all throughout the day, and it’s just going to distract us from our goals. That smoker example from earlier, they want a smoke after they’re done working because they associate driving with smoking. Their brain has made that connection. The room we’re in matters, the time of day matters, who’s in the room with you matters.

I hope we can see that motivation is not the problem, willpower is not the problem, we might just need to establish some habits that are in alignment with our vision and with our goals. Making indie game is hard, and we’ve got enough things to worry about already. We don’t need to be fighting with our subconscious mind every step if the way too, because that’s just going to be exhausting.

Start from small babits. Don’t waste 2024!

BTW, everything that I have discussed it’s all available in much more detail in the book , I strongly recommend you read this book in the free time.

今天有人吐槽我“不是财务自由了吗，干嘛还在搞独立游戏？”。

这是一个有意思的问题，值得我认真解释回答一下。“如果实现了财务自由，就不应该主动工作赚钱”，这其实是对财务自由的一个典型的误解。我们先看看维基百科中对财务自由的定义：“财务自由是指你工作并不为了钱，而钱完全够用的状态。只有资产产生的被动收入等于或超过所需的日常开支，就可以算作进入财务自由的状态。此时只要不发生什么重大灾难，就可以生活下去，你可以继续工作下去，或选择自己真正喜欢做的事，而不是为生活所迫做那些不乐意却又无可奈何要做的事。”
